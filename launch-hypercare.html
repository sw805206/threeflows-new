Our approach

Launch is the most exciting phase — the first time the rubber actually hits the road. It’s also where the task list explodes, the timeline slips, and the same problems keep coming back because nobody had time to fix the cause.

Most firms will offer to do it all for you. Having been there ourselves, we don’t think that’s what you want. You started this because you want to be in charge of it — but being in charge alone is hard. What you need is someone in it with you: real support on the actual work, and honest support on the days it gets heavy.

That’s what a runningmate is. Not a cold consultant handing over a report — more like a coach who runs the course beside you. Someone who shares the founder’s drive but keeps a clear head, who’s disciplined when you’re stretched, and who’s seen this stretch of road before.

Painpoints & the solves

The painpoints
  • Not clear what’s needed, or what comes first — so the timeline drags and slips
  • Too many tasks, too little time, all landing at once — and the stress starts affecting the work
  • Underestimating how connected everything is — one wrong setup causing problems downstream
  • Stuck in firefight mode, with the same issues repeating after launch
The solves
  • A launch playbook and project plan — what happens, in what order
  • On-call problem-solving sessions when something needs sorting today
  • A weekly meet-up to recap progress and stay ahead of risk
  • A post-launch package: process, systems, and improvement, documented

Frequently asked questions

When should I start launch support?
Earlier than most people think — ideally we join you from day one, at plan and source. Otherwise, no later than a month before you set up the company or place the first purchase order.
What if something goes wrong at the worst possible time?
That’s what the weekly recap (proactive) and the hotline (reactive) are for. We constantly scan the work to minimize risk and prepare for the worst case — and when it hits, we know what to do.
How long does launch support run?
Typically three to six months, depending on how much is being set up and how fast the first cycle moves. We agree the shape at the discovery call.
What if I’ve already started and just need help with part of it?
That’s fine — we can join mid-process. Common entry points are a pre-shipment inspection briefing, a customs and compliance review, or emergency support when something has already gone wrong. We scope a targeted engagement rather than the full program. Tell us where you are and we’ll tell you what makes sense.
What kind of operating system should I implement on day one?
If you’re launching with a handful of products, start with Google Sheets — almost every business should. A spreadsheet forces you to understand your data before you automate it, and it’s far cheaper to get wrong. Once you’ve validated your processes and know what the system actually needs to do, we can build something more permanent. Skipping that step usually produces an expensive system built on processes you don’t fully understand yet.
